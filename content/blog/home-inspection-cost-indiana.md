## What Affects the Cost?

Several factors influence inspection pricing:

### Home Size

Larger homes take longer to inspect. A 1,200 square foot ranch in Fremont takes less time than a 3,500 square foot home in Auburn. Most inspections take 2-4 hours.

### Home Age

Older homes — common throughout Noble County and DeKalb County — often require more thorough evaluation. Homes built before 1970 may have outdated wiring, aging plumbing, or foundation concerns that take extra time to document.

### Property Type

New construction inspections involve checking builder workmanship and code compliance, which requires a different approach than inspecting an existing home. Lakefront properties in Steuben County need additional attention to moisture, septic, and foundation concerns.

### Additional Services

Some buyers request additional testing beyond the standard inspection:

- Radon testing
- Mold inspection
- Termite and pest inspection
- Septic system inspection

These services are typically performed by specialized providers and are priced separately.

## Is a Home Inspection Worth the Cost?

A home inspection costs a few hundred dollars on a purchase that is likely six figures. Here is why it pays for itself:

### Negotiating Power

Inspection findings give you leverage. If the report identifies a roof nearing end of life or an aging HVAC system, you can negotiate a lower price or request repairs before closing. A single finding can save you thousands.

### Avoiding Surprises

Hidden problems — foundation cracks, electrical hazards, plumbing leaks behind walls — can cost $5,000 to $50,000+ to repair. An inspection catches what you cannot see with your own eyes.

### Peace of Mind

Knowing the true condition of the property lets you make a confident decision. Whether you move forward, renegotiate, or walk away, you are making an informed choice.

## Can I Skip the Inspection to Save Money?

Some buyers in competitive markets consider waiving the inspection. This is risky. Skipping a $350 inspection on a $200,000 home to save less than 0.2% of the purchase price leaves you exposed to potentially massive repair costs.
