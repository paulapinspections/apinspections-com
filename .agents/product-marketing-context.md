# Product Marketing Context

*Last updated: 2026-03-25*

## Product Overview
**One-liner:** AP Inspections provides thorough, professional home inspection services in northeast Indiana.
**What it does:** AP Inspections performs comprehensive residential property inspections — covering structural components, electrical, plumbing, HVAC, roofing, and safety concerns — and delivers same-day detailed reports with photo documentation. Services include pre-purchase, pre-listing, and new construction inspections.
**Product category:** Home inspection services
**Product type:** Local service business
**Business model:** Per-inspection pricing. Introductory pricing starting from $300. Pre-Purchase ($350), Pre-Listing ($325), New Construction ($400). General range $300–$500 depending on property size and type.

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** Buying a home is the largest financial decision most people make, and they can't see what's behind the walls. Hidden defects — bad wiring, foundation cracks, roof damage, plumbing issues — can cost tens of thousands of dollars after closing.
**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Skipping the inspection entirely (risky — leads to expensive surprises)
- Relying on the seller's disclosure (incomplete, often misses major issues)
- Using a cheap or rushed inspector (misses critical problems, vague reports)
- National franchise inspectors (impersonal, may not know local building codes and conditions)
**What it costs them:** Thousands in unexpected repairs, failed negotiations, deals falling through, stress and regret
**Emotional tension:** Fear of making a huge financial mistake. Anxiety about what they can't see. Worry that they'll close on a money pit. Sellers fear deals collapsing at the last minute over inspection findings.

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct competitors:** Aardvark Home Inspections (Angola, IN) — aggressively marketing for new clients in the area. Other local home inspectors in Steuben, Noble, Lagrange, and Dekalb counties.
- Falls short: Many don't offer same-day reports, less thorough documentation, harder to schedule
**Secondary competitors:** General contractors or handymen doing informal property assessments
- Falls short: Not licensed inspectors, no standardized report, miss systemic issues
**Indirect competitors:** Waiving the inspection contingency (increasingly pressured in competitive markets)
- Falls short: Extremely risky — buyers have zero protection against hidden defects
